Vital News in Language Revitalization

Historic National Plan Released

The Biden-Harris Administration released a 10-Year National Plan on Native Language Revitalization, calling for a **$16.7 billion** investment to support language nests, immersion schools, and mentor-apprentice programs across the U.S.

Read Details

AI and Technology Boost

New projects are leveraging **AI and hologram technology** (like the Na-Cho Nyäk Dun Holopresence Initiative and Dartmouth's NüshuRescue) to document, preserve, and create resources for "low-resource" endangered languages like Northern Tutchone.

First Nations/Métis Funding Autonomy (Canada)

The Canadian government announced a new distinction-based funding model to allocate funds **directly to First Nations and Métis Peoples** for self-led language strategies, including over $15.3 million for Michif revitalization.

About the Indigenous Voices Archive

This archive is a digital repository built by and for Native American communities, dedicated to preserving, promoting, and making accessible the vital linguistic heritage of North America.

Our mission is to support language keepers, educators, and learners by centralizing resources, advocating for self-determination in linguistic efforts, and providing a platform free from colonial terms or bias.

We are grateful to the many nations and individuals who have contributed to the resources listed here. If you wish to **submit a resource** or partnership proposal, please use the contact form below.
