Welcome to the Archive

Dedicated to the preservation and promotion of Indigenous languages, cultures, and oral histories. Explore our growing collection of stories and resources.

Our mission is to provide a platform for Indigenous voices to share their heritage and ensure the survival of linguistic diversity for future generations.

Begin your journey by navigating through the links above to discover authentic narratives and valuable educational materials.

Educational Resources & Databases

A curated list of external resources, museums, and databases dedicated to Indigenous language preservation and cultural heritage. Scroll down to see the full list.

Native Languages of the Americas (California Section)

Small non-profit dedicated to language survival through internet technology. Provides accessible vocabulary and cultural background.

Coverage: California Languages

Visit Website

California State Parks - California Indians Language

Educational resource documenting California's 12,000-year cultural heritage and sixty-four distinct indigenous languages.

Coverage: California Languages

Visit Website

Cherokee Nation Language Platform

Cherokee Nation's official online language learning platform.

Coverage: Eastern Woodlands (Cherokee)

Visit Website

Nez Perce/Sahaptin Language Resources

Resources maintained by Yakama Nation, University of Alaska, and National Park Service, promoting the preservation of the Nimipuutímt language.

Coverage: Plateau (Nez Perce, Sahaptin)

Visit National Park Service Resource

Secwepemc Museum & Heritage Park

Plateau cultural museum maintaining linguistic recordings, historical documents, and cultural materials for research and education.

Coverage: Plateau (Secwepemctsin)

Visit Museum Website

Sam Noble Museum

Extensive Native American Languages Collection with 175 languages represented through audio/video recordings, manuscripts, books, and curricula.

Coverage: Plains (175 Native American Languages)

Visit Collections

Museum of Indian Arts and Culture

World-renowned museum centering indigenous perspectives and voices of Pueblo, Navajo (Diné), and Apache cultures of the Southwest, featuring Native scholarship and oral histories.

Coverage: Southwest (Pueblo, Navajo, Apache Languages)

Visit Website

Heard Museum

Major museum with 12 galleries of American Indian art, over 40,000 items including library and archives, focusing on Southwest Indigenous cultures.

Coverage: Southwest Languages

Visit Website

Indian Pueblo Cultural Center

Gateway to the 19 Pueblos with a museum and exhibition galleries featuring Pueblo culture, murals by Pueblo artists and cultural programs.

Coverage: Pueblo Languages

Visit Website
